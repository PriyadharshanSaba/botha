01

Introduction

Bodha Ventures LLP ("Bodha", "we", "our", "us") is a financial services and education company headquartered in Bengaluru, India. We are fully committed to protecting your personal data and maintaining transparent practices in how it is collected, processed, and safeguarded.

This Privacy Policy applies to all users ("you", "your") who access or interact with our digital properties and services, including:

  • Personal Finance Courses (with user accounts and login functionality)
  • Virtual CFO (vCFO) advisory services
  • Venture Capital activities (internal capital deployment)
  • Financial tools and calculators
  • Blogs and educational content

By using our website or services, you acknowledge and agree to the practices described in this Policy. This document is legally binding. If you do not agree, please discontinue use of our platform.

Compliance

We operate in full compliance with the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (DPDP Act), and other applicable Indian data protection laws.

02

Information We Collect

We collect only the minimum personal data necessary to deliver and improve our services. We do not engage in excessive or speculative data collection.

A. Personal Information

Collected when you register, purchase, or contact us:

  • Full Name
  • Email Address
  • Phone Number
  • Billing Address
  • Payment details (processed exclusively via PCI-DSS compliant third-party gateways; we do not store card data)

B. Account & Usage Data

For course users and logged-in members:

  • Login credentials (stored using industry-standard hashing — never in plain text)
  • Course progress, completion status, and activity timestamps
  • Platform preferences and saved configurations
  • Interactions with tools, calculators, and content

C. Financial & Business Information (vCFO Clients Only)

Sensitive Data Notice

Financial and business data shared in the context of vCFO engagements is treated with the highest level of confidentiality. It is governed by a separate engagement agreement and is never used for marketing, analytics, or shared with any third party absent a legal obligation.

  • Business financial records and statements
  • Transactional data shared for advisory purposes
  • Reports, projections, and documents provided by you

D. Automatically Collected Technical Data

  • IP address and approximate geographic location
  • Browser type, version, and device information
  • Pages visited, navigation paths, and time spent
  • Referral URLs and session identifiers
  • Cookie and tracking technology data (see Section 8)

E. Content Access & Security Audit Logs

For logged-in course users, we maintain an append-only audit log of chapter views. Each record captures the account identifier, the module and chapter accessed, the timestamp, the originating IP address, and the user-agent string of the browser used.

This log is used strictly for security, fraud prevention, and protection of our intellectual property — for example, to investigate suspected unauthorised redistribution of paid course material. It is not used for marketing, profiling, or behavioural analytics.

Legal Basis for Processing

Under the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023, we process your personal data on the following lawful grounds:

  • Consent — For sign-ups, optional marketing communications, and non-essential cookies. You may withdraw consent at any time without affecting the lawfulness of prior processing.
  • Contractual Necessity — For delivering courses, managing accounts, processing payments, and fulfilling vCFO service obligations.
  • Legitimate Use — For fraud prevention, platform security, and improving our services, where such use does not override your fundamental rights.
  • Legal Obligation — Where processing is required to comply with applicable Indian law, a court order, or regulatory authority.
Your Control

Where processing is based on consent, you may withdraw it at any time by contacting us at the email listed in Section 15. Withdrawal does not affect the legality of processing that occurred before withdrawal.

05

Data Sharing & Disclosure

We Do Not Sell Your Data

Bodha Ventures LLP does not sell, rent, trade, or monetise your personal data in any form, to any party, for any purpose.

We may share your data only in the following limited circumstances:

A. Trusted Service Providers

We engage third-party vendors solely to support our operations. We require, and endeavour to ensure by contract or equivalent written understanding, that all vendors handle your personal data only for the specific purpose for which it was shared, and refrain from any independent use, disclosure, or retention beyond what is strictly necessary.

Where formal Data Processing Agreements (DPAs) are not yet in place with a particular vendor, we rely on such vendors' own published privacy commitments and applicable terms of service, and are in the process of formalising data protection obligations contractually.

  • Payment gateways (for transaction processing — governed by their own PCI-DSS compliance and privacy terms)
  • Cloud hosting providers (for platform infrastructure)
  • Analytics platforms (using anonymised or aggregated data only, where possible)
  • Communication tools (for transactional emails)
Third-Party Limitation

While we take reasonable steps to select reputable vendors and limit data shared with them, Bodha Ventures LLP cannot guarantee the independent data practices of third-party service providers. Please refer to Section 16 (Limitation of Liability) for our position on third-party incidents.

B. Legal & Regulatory Authorities

We will disclose data if required by a valid court order, law enforcement request, or regulatory mandate under Indian law. We will, where legally permissible, notify you of any such request.

C. Business Transfers

In the event of a merger, acquisition, or restructuring of Bodha Ventures LLP, personal data may be transferred to the successor entity, subject to equivalent privacy protections and your notification.

06

Data Security

We implement layered, industry-standard security measures to protect your personal data against unauthorised access, disclosure, alteration, or destruction:

  • SSL/TLS encryption for all data in transit
  • Encrypted storage for sensitive data at rest
  • Hashed and salted password storage (passwords are never stored in plain text)
  • Role-based access controls — only authorised personnel access personal data
  • Regular security audits and vulnerability assessments
  • Incident response procedures for potential data breaches
Important Notice

No system is entirely immune to security risks. In the event of a data breach affecting your rights or interests, we will notify you and the relevant authority as required under the DPDP Act, 2023.

Data Retention

We retain your personal data only for as long as necessary to fulfil the purpose for which it was collected, or as required by applicable law.

Data Category Retention Period

Course progress and engagement history Duration of account + 12 months post-deletion

Your Rights

Under the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023, and applicable Indian law, you have the following rights with respect to your personal data:

Right to Access
Request a summary of the personal data we hold about you and how it is being used.
Right to Correction
Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal data we hold about you.
Right to Erasure
Request deletion of your personal data, subject to legal retention obligations.
Right to Withdraw Consent
Withdraw consent to processing at any time, without affecting past lawful processing.
Right to Grievance Redressal
Lodge a complaint with our Grievance Officer (Section 14) or the Data Protection Board of India.
Right to Nominate
Nominate an individual to exercise your data rights on your behalf in the event of your death or incapacity.

To exercise any of the above rights, please contact us at info@bodhaventures.in. We will respond within 30 days of receiving a valid request.

You are solely responsible for maintaining the confidentiality of your account credentials. Bodha Ventures LLP shall not be held liable for any unauthorised access to your account resulting from your failure to secure your login details, or from sharing your credentials with third parties.

C. Force Majeure & Unforeseen Events

We shall not be liable for any data incidents arising from circumstances beyond our reasonable control, including but not limited to cyberattacks by state or non-state actors, natural disasters, telecommunications failures, or government-mandated actions.

D. Cap on Liability — Paid Courses

To the maximum extent permitted by applicable Indian law, Bodha Ventures LLP's total aggregate liability to you for all claims arising out of or in connection with a paid course or service — whether arising from one incident or multiple incidents — shall not exceed the amount actually paid by you for that specific transaction. For a one-year Personal Finance Course priced at ₹999 (Indian Rupees Nine Hundred and Ninety-Nine), the maximum cumulative liability across all claims combined is therefore ₹999, regardless of the number, nature, or frequency of claims raised.

E. Cap on Liability — Free Services

For users accessing free services — including financial calculators, blog content, or any other no-charge features — Bodha Ventures LLP's total liability shall not exceed ₹500 (Indian Rupees Five Hundred) per incident. As these services carry no financial consideration, this cap reflects the nature of the relationship. Users of free services are additionally directed to Section 15 (Educational Disclaimer), which governs reliance on such content.

F. Time Bar on Claims

Any claim arising out of or in connection with this Privacy Policy or your use of our platform must be raised in writing within 90 (ninety) days of the date on which you became aware, or reasonably ought to have become aware, of the event giving rise to the claim. Claims raised after this period are expressly time-barred.

G. No Liability for Indirect Loss

In no event shall Bodha Ventures LLP be liable for any indirect, incidental, consequential, special, or punitive damages — including loss of profits, loss of data, loss of goodwill, or business interruption — even if we have been advised of the possibility of such damages.
